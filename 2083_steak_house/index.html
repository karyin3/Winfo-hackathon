Composting Basics

Start from home!

Composting can divert as much as 30% of household waste away from the garbage. It helps to aerate soil and breaks down organic material for plant use and wards off plant disease. It is a natural alternative to chemical fertilizers.

Three basic ingredients

Browns

Includes materials such as dead leaves, branches, and twigs.

Greens

Includes materials such as grass clippings, vegetable waste, fruit scraps and coffee grounds.

Water

Right amount of water, greens, and browns is essential for compost development.

Your compost pile should have equal amount of browns and greens.

Recycling

The act of repurposing reusable materials

Know the numbers!

Plastic #1 - Polyethylene Terephthalate

Usually clear. Found in soda bottles, water bottles, beer bottles etc.

Recycled into tote bags, furniture, carpet, paneling, fiber, and polar fleece.

Plastic #2 - High Density Polyethylene

Usually opaque. Found in milk jugs, household cleaner containers, juice bottles, shampoo bottles etc.

Recycled into pens, recycling containers, detergent bottles

Plastic #3 - Polyvinyl Chloride

Used to make food wrap, plumbing pipes and detergent bottles etc.

Recycled into paneling, flooring, speed bumps, decks

Plastic #4 - Low Density Polyethylene

Found in squeezable bottles, shopping bags, clothing, carpet etc.

Recycled into compost bins, trash can liners and cans.

Plastic #5 - Polypropylene (Styrofoam)

Found in yogurt containers, ketchup bottles, syrup bottles and medicine bottles

Recycled into brooms, auto battery cases, bins

Plastic #6 - Polystyrene

Difficult to recycle. Found in compact disc cases, egg cartons, disposable plates and cups.

Plastic #7 - Other

All plastic resins that do not fit into other categories

Recycled into plastic lumber and other custom-made products

Landfill Crisis

The average person generates over 4 pounds of trash every day and about 1.5 tons of solid waste per year. Americans make more than 200 million tons of garbage each year, enough to fill Busch Stadium from top to bottom twice a day.

Landfills cause pollution to the local environment by contaminating the groundwater and aquifers, contaminating the soil, and producing methane. Methane is a greenhouse gas that is 21 times more powerful than carbon dioxide (CO2). Greenhouse gasses are said to be the leading cause of global warming. Landfill methane is created when organic materials (such as food scraps, paper, and yard waste) decompose.
